Qourma recipe

Description

Korma or qorma is a dish originating in South Asia, consisting of meat or vegetables braised with yogurt, water or stock, and spices to produce a thick sauce or gravy

Ingredietnts

    To roast and grind to a paste

  • 4-6 Clove
  • 1 inch Cinnamon
  • 6-8 Black Peppercorns
  • 3-4 Green Cardamom
  • 2 Black Cardamom
  • 10-12 Cashew Nuts
  • 1 cup Browned Onion

    • For the curry

  • 3 tablespoon Ghee
  • 3 tablespoon Oil
  • 1 kg Mutton (Curry cut)
  • 4 teaspoon Ginger Garlic Green Chilli Paste
  • 1 cup Curd (Whisked with 1 tablespoon maida.)
  • 2 tablespoon Coriander Powder
  • 2 teaspoon Kashmiri Red Chilli Powder
  • teaspoon Turmeric Powder
  • teaspoon Cumin Powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 10-12 strands Saffron (Soaked in 2 tablespoon water.)
  • 2-3 drops Kewra Extract

Steps

  1. Roast all the whole spices and cashew nuts until they turn slightly browned and fragrant.
  2. Add them in a blender along with fried onion and add 1 tablespoon water to make a coarse paste.
  3. Heat oil and ghee in a pressure cooker.
  4. Add mutton pieces and fry on high heat for 8-10 minutes.
  5. Add the masala paste and ginger garlic paste and fry for 3-4 minutes.
  6. Now add yogurt, coriander powder, red chilli powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder and salt and fry for 3-4 minutes.
  7. Add 2 cups of water and pressure cook until mutton is cooked well.
  8. Remove the cooker from heat and let the pressure release.
  9. Add saffron soaked in water and kewra essence and mix well.
  10. Add more water if required and cook for some more time.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot.
